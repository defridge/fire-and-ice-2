About Us

We opened our doors in August of 2023 with one goal. To provide a space for our community to come and enjoy doing their contrast therapy in a friendly and safe environment. Join us on your journey of better physical and mental health through the power of Fire and Ice. Our sessions are 40 minutes long, and are guaranteed to leave you feeling relaxed and revitalised. Will you take the plunge today ? Take the first step to better health. Your body and mind will thank you for it. See you in the cold !

Our Story

Hi, My name is Niall, and I am the proud owner of Fire and Ice Recovery here in donaghmede. My journey into the cold started back in 2021 when a friend of mine introduced me to sea swimming during the winter months. I never understood why all these crazy people were getting into the icey water down at Portmarnock beach, even the thoughts of it was enough to send a chill up your spine, but when I tried it for myself, I will never forget that feeling ! First, the pain of the cold water, followed by the burning of my body as it tried to stay warm. The real buzz came after when we got back out, talk about feeling alive ! It was absolutely exhilarating. My next venture was the sauna and how the mix of both hot and cold together brought a whole new level to the overall experience. The contrast between both hot and cold was a match made in heaven. I was addicted ! It was then when I really delved into the science behind why it made me feel so good, physically and mentally. When a space became available in my hometown of Donaghmede, I knew what I wanted to do. So, in August of 2023, we opened our doors with one goal in mind. Bringing this feeling to our community. We now provide a space for people to come and do their contrast therapy in a safe and friendly environment, and leave feeling so much better than they arrived. So, are you going to take the plunge today ?

Benefits

Understand the benefits of choosing Fire and Ice Recovery.

  1. Improved Cardiovascular Health: Regular sauna use can improve circulation, reduce blood pressure, and enhance heart health. The heat causes blood vessels to dilate, which improves blood flow and reduces the strain on the heart.

  2. Detoxification: Sweating in a sauna helps remove toxins from the body, including heavy metals like lead, cadmium, arsenic, and mercury, through perspiration.

  3. Relaxation and Stress Reduction: The heat from the sauna relaxes muscles, alleviates tension, and promotes relaxation, which can reduce stress levels. This environment can also promote the release of endorphins, the body’s natural “feel-good” chemicals.

  4. Skin Health: Sweating opens up pores, helping to cleanse the skin and remove dead skin cells, which can improve skin health and appearance.

  5. Pain Relief: Sauna use can relieve muscle and joint pain by increasing blood circulation and relaxing muscles. It can be beneficial for people with arthritis and other chronic pain conditions.

  6. Improved Immune Function: Regular sauna use can enhance the immune system by stimulating the production of white blood cells, which helps fight off infections.

  7. Respiratory Benefits: Inhalation of hot air can help improve respiratory function and alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions like asthma, bronchitis, and allergies.

  1. Enhanced Recovery and Performance: Alternating between hot and cold can boost recovery and improve physical performance by combining the benefits of muscle relaxation and inflammation reduction.

  2. Improved Circulation: The rapid alternation between heat and cold stimulates blood flow and enhances the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to muscles, which aids in recovery.

  3. Mental Resilience: The contrast between hot and cold exposure can strengthen mental resilience, as the body learns to adapt quickly to changing conditions.

  4. Detoxification and Immune Function: The combination can enhance detoxification processes and improve immune function more effectively than either therapy alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

We ask that everyone bring two towels, a small one to sit on in the sauna and one to dry yourself with. You will also need your bathing suits or trunks and a bottle of water to stay hydrated.

The minimum age is 16 years old.

Cold exposure and sauna are completely safe once done correctly and in a safe environment. We do recommend that everyone reads our waiver for all the relevant medical advice or contact your doctor before coming if you fall into any of the categories that would put you at risk.
